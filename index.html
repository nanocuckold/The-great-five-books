The great five books of Dostoevsky

The greatest books in Russian literature

Release date:

  • 1866 - Crime and Punishment, socio-psychological and socio-philosophical novel.
  • 1868 - Idiot, He was one of the most beloved works of the writer, most fully expressing both the moral and philosophical position of Dostoevsky and his artistic principles in the 1860s.
  • 1871—1872 - Demons, One of the most politicized novels of Dostoevsky was written by him under the impression of the germs of terrorist and radical movements among the Russian intellectuals, commoners and others.
  • 1875 - The Adolescent, In the novel, the writer touches on the problem of "fathers and children", examining it through the relationship of the protagonist, a teenager Arkady Makarovich Dolgoruky, with his father Andrei Petrovich Versilov.
  • 1879—1880 - The Brothers Karamazov, the novel raises deep questions: about God, freedom, morality. Dostoevsky conceived the novel as the first part of the epic novel The Story of the Great Sinner. The work was completed in November 1880. The writer died two months after publication.
